184 kcal. The Seed Nobody Counts.

Jackfruit seeds are the lowest-calorie, lowest-fat seed you can eat, with a resistant starch profile no super seed can match — and most of the world throws them away.

Click the 184 stat to see how jackfruit repositions against starches instead of seeds.

Walk into any health food store and the seed hierarchy is settled: chia for fiber, hemp for protein, flax for omega-3. But the world's largest tree fruit produces seeds that get thrown away as agricultural waste — 15% of every harvest. Those seeds have been eaten for centuries across South and Southeast Asia. At 184 calories, 0.6g fat, and 30% resistant starch, they don't belong in the super seed category at all. They belong in a category of their own.

~30% of the starch is resistant starch — the kind that feeds gut bacteria and regulates blood sugar.

— Antioxidant Profile

Where Jackfruit Wins — and Loses

The comparison isn't "which seed is best" — it's "what role does each play." Jackfruit seeds dominate on cost, calories, fat, and gut health compounds. They lose on protein, healthy fats, fiber volume, convenience, and availability.

Where Jackfruit Wins

Calorie Density: 184 vs 486+
Lowest by a 300 kcal margin. The only seed that qualifies as low-calorie.
Fat Content: 0.6g vs 31-51g
A 50x difference from the next lowest. Effectively zero fat.
Resistant Starch: ~30%
No other seed has a meaningful resistant starch fraction.
Antioxidants: Berry-Level
Catechin, ferulic acid, epicatechin, rutin, gallic acid — polyphenols you'd expect from a berry.
Cost: Free (or close)
Agricultural waste in tropical regions. Compare to chia at $8-15/lb.

Where Jackfruit Loses

Protein: 7g vs 30-32g
Not even close. Hemp and pumpkin seeds are 4-5x higher.
Healthy Fats: None
No omega-3, no omega-6, no monounsaturated. Every other seed is a significant fat source.
Total Fiber: 3.8g vs 27-34g
Flax and chia dominate. Jackfruit's fiber story is specifically resistant starch.
Convenience: Cooking Required
20-30 min boiling or roasting. Every other seed is grab-and-go.
Availability: Tropical Only
Hard to find outside Asian groceries. Supply chain barely exists in the West.

The Unique Play: Resistant Starch

Resistant starch passes through to the large intestine undigested — it feeds beneficial gut bacteria, produces short-chain fatty acids, improves insulin sensitivity, and increases satiety. Most starchy foods have 1-5% resistant starch. Jackfruit seeds have 30%. This is the single most distinctive nutritional fact about jackfruit seeds.

Resistant starch as % of total starch. Jackfruit seeds at ~30% are 3-10x higher than any common carb source.

The Honest Reframe: Seeds vs Starches

Jackfruit seeds aren't competing with super seeds. They're competing with potatoes, rice, chickpeas, and lentils. Against that comparison, they dominate on resistant starch and antioxidants while holding competitive protein. Who Should Care — and Who Shouldn't

Jackfruit seeds aren't for everyone. That's the honest framing. Here's who benefits and who should look elsewhere.

Should Care

People in Tropical Regions
You're literally throwing these away. The no-brainer case.
Blood Sugar Managers
The resistant starch profile is genuinely distinctive and evidence-backed for insulin sensitivity.
Low-Fat Dieters
The only seed that qualifies as a low-fat food. 0.6g per 100g.
Gut Health Optimizers
Resistant starch + prebiotic compounds is a strong combination for microbiome health.
Sustainability Advocates
Eating agricultural waste that's nutritionally dense. The sustainability argument writes itself.

Shouldn't Pivot

Protein Optimizers
Eat hemp or pumpkin seeds instead. 30-32g vs 7g — not competitive.
Omega-3 Seekers
Eat chia or flax instead. Jackfruit seeds have zero omega-3.
Convenience Seekers
The 20-30 min cooking requirement is a real daily barrier.
Outside Tropical Regions
Availability makes this impractical as a daily staple — for now.
